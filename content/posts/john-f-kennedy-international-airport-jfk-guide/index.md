John F. Kennedy International Airport, commonly referred to as JFK, is located in New York, United States. It serves as a major gateway for international and domestic air travel. The airport is situated at coordinates 40.642334 latitude and -73.78817 longitude, placing it within the busy metropolitan area of New York City. JFK operates in the America/New_York timezone, aligning with the local time observed in the region.

As one of the busiest airports in the United States, JFK plays a crucial role in connecting travelers to various destinations across the globe. The airport's extensive network facilitates a wide range of travel options for passengers, whether they are flying domestically or internationally.

## Getting To and From the Airport

When planning your journey to and from JFK, there are several transportation options available. Travelers can utilize taxis, ride-sharing services, or public transportation to reach the airport. It is advisable to consider the time of day and potential traffic conditions when selecting your mode of transport, as these factors can significantly impact travel times.

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/john-f-kennedy-international-airport-jfk-guide/body_4.jpg)

For those opting for public transportation, there are various options that connect the airport to different parts of New York City. Buses and trains provide convenient access, allowing travelers to navigate the city efficiently. 

## Practical Tips for Travelers

When traveling through JFK, it is essential to arrive at the airport well in advance of your flight. Security lines can be lengthy, especially during peak travel times, so allocating extra time for check-in and security screening is prudent.
