Jeju International Airport, identified by the IATA code CJU, serves as the primary gateway to Jeju Island in [South Korea](https://visafree.techpawz.com/posts/south-korea-visa-free/). Positioned at coordinates 33.5067, 126.49312, the airport operates within the [Asia](https://esim.techpawz.com/posts/esim-asia/)/[Seoul](https://flights.techpawz.com/posts/cheapest-flights-miami-to-seoul/) timezone. It plays a crucial role in connecting travelers to this popular destination, known for its natural beauty and unique cultural offerings. 

The airport is relatively small compared to major international hubs, but it is equipped to handle a steady flow of passengers, particularly those traveling to and from the mainland. As a key entry point for tourists and business travelers alike, Jeju International Airport aims to provide a seamless travel experience.

Jeju International Airport is served by two airlines: Asiana Airlines and Korean Air. Both of these carriers offer full-service options, catering to various travel needs. While the airport has limited airline representation, these two airlines are well-established in the region and provide reliable service to their passengers.

## Direct Destinations from CJU

Currently, Jeju International Airport offers direct flights to one destination, which is Gimpo International Airport (GMP). This route is essential for travelers looking to connect between Jeju Island and the busy capital of South Korea, Seoul. The limited number of direct destinations may restrict travel options, but it reflects the airport's focus on serving its primary market effectively.
